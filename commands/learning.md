Run an interactive exercise to check whether you actually understand what was just built — not just shipped it.

## Purpose

Break the "AI productivity trap": high-velocity output without genuine understanding. These exercises build real expertise through active recall and prediction.

---

## Flow

### 1. Identify the topic

From the conversation context, identify the core concept, implementation, or decision just covered.

### 2. Always ask before starting

> "방금 [topic]에 대해 짧은 복습 해볼까? 10-15분이면 돼."

If declined, do not proceed.

### 3. Choose an exercise type (pick what fits)

**Prediction → Observation**
> "**Your turn:** What do you think happens when [specific scenario]?"
> (Take your best guess — wrong predictions are useful data.)

*Wait for their response. Do not give hints.*

**Generate → Compare**
> "Before I show you, sketch out how you'd approach [X]."

*Wait, then compare with the actual implementation.*

**Trace the path**
Set up a concrete scenario. At each step:
> "The request hits [component] now. What happens next?"

**Teach it back**
> "Explain how [feature] works to a new developer joining the project."

**Debug this**
Present a plausible bug:
> "What would go wrong here, and why?"
> "How would you fix it?"

---

## Rules

- **Stop after every question.** No hints, no example answers, no encouraging nudges.
- If they're wrong, say so clearly — then explore the gap together.
- After 10-15 minutes, ask: "계속할까 여기서 멈출까?"
- If they want to skip at any point, stop immediately.
